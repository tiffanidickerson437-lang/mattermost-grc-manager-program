# Days 61 to 90: Deliver and institutionalize

Objective: hand over metric 1 complete, convert the pilot into the program's normal operating mode, and put the team's growth case in front of leadership with data behind it.

---

## Workstream A: Deliver metric 1 (the anchor)

The day-90 deliverable, as recalibrated in week one, lands here:

| Component | Content |
|---|---|
| Gap assessment | The full Level 2 self-assessment results from phase two: score, dispositions, evidence index, and the honest gap list |
| Readiness roadmap | Regime-aware, built on the fork the [plan overview](README.md) defines. Whatever the Task Force reported in September, the roadmap names the branch we are on, the evidence standard we hold either way, and dated milestones to close every POA&M item |
| The affirmation package | Who signs the SPRS affirmation, on what evidence, on what refresh cadence, held to the standard that nothing gets affirmed which cannot be re-derived on demand |

Presented to security leadership as a walkthrough, with the federal subsidiary's leadership in the room if the boundary decision put their entity in scope.

## Workstream B: Pilot to production (metric 3)

- Extend continuous monitoring from the pilot family to the two or three families the audit calendar says matter next.
- Publish the counting stat: how many evidence items are now computed on schedule versus collected by hand, before and after. This number is the headline of the 90-day report and the baseline for the next quarter's target.
- Write the pattern down as a runbook so extending to the next family is repetition, no longer invention.

## Workstream C: Customer assurance at steady state (metric 4)

Full procedure in the [runbook](../05-stakeholder-management/customer-assurance-pass.md). By day 90:

- The questionnaire answer bank reviewed end to end, every answer traceable to a control or an evidence artifact, review-gated so future edits get a second set of eyes.
- Trust center contents current, with an owner and refresh cadence per document.
- The public claims-consistency findings from phase one resolved or formally routed, including retiring the FedRAMP vocabulary that changed in May.
- Turnaround measured: time from questionnaire arrival to response, so the deal-cycle metric has a number attached.

## Workstream D: The team proposal (metric 5)

The growth case, built from 90 days of measured reality rather than assertion:

1. Volume data: questionnaires handled, evidence items automated versus manual, audit calendar load, POA&M count and burn rate.
2. What automation is already absorbing, and what it structurally cannot (risk acceptance, auditor relationships, judgment calls on scoping).
3. The first-hire profile and trigger condition, sequenced against the roadmap. If the data says the trigger is not near, the proposal says that too.

## Workstream E: The 90-day report and adopted policies

- AI usage policy adopted (drafted in phase two, through review by now).
- Vulnerability-process control family merged into the control library.
- The 90-day report: one document for my manager and their leadership covering the five metrics by number, each with its artifact linked, plus the next-quarter commitments. The report's own form makes the argument: every claim in it is checkable, which is what the whole program is supposed to feel like.

## Day-90 exit criteria

- [ ] Metric 1 delivered and walked through with leadership
- [ ] Continuous monitoring live on multiple control families with a published manual-work-retired count
- [ ] Questionnaire bank evidence-linked and review-gated; trust center owned and current
- [ ] Team proposal presented with volume data
- [ ] AI usage policy adopted; vulnerability control family in the library
- [ ] 90-day report delivered; next-quarter targets agreed

## What day 91 looks like

The function runs on a cadence: evidence computes on schedule, drift opens tickets, audits consume artifacts that already exist, and the roadmap adjusts when the regulators move again. If the first 90 days went well, day 91 should feel routine, and that routineness is the whole point of the plan.
