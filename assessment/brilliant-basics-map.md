On 13 July 2026 the DoW CIO suspended CMMC Phase II. The same office
publishes **[Brilliant at the Basics](https://dowcio.war.gov/BrilliantBasics/)**
— a Top 10 of IT practices it tells the Defense Industrial Base to
actually do, framed explicitly as stripping away *"administrative
complexity and compliance overhead."*

It is not a framework. It carries no assessment, appears in no contract
clause, and nobody will ever audit against it. It is something more
useful: the clearest public statement of what the customer thinks good
looks like — published by the office that just took the credential away.

So it is worth asking what the enforced baseline can see of it.

## The finding — practice 08: Secure AI Adoption and Data Protection

> Establish clear policies and technical guardrails governing the use of artificial intelligence and automation tools across your workforce. Explicitly prohibit the input of sensitive Department data into public, commercial AI systems. By implementing content filtering, endpoint controls, and approved enterprise AI environments, you prevent accidental data exposure while enabling the safe adoption of advanced technologies.

**NIST SP 800-171 Rev 2 contains no artificial-intelligence
requirement.** Not a weak one — none. Rev 2 was published in
February 2020; its 110 requirements predate the workforce LLM
entirely, and Rev 3 does not add one either.

So a contractor can score a perfect **110** in SPRS, sign the
[32 CFR 170.22](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-32/subtitle-A/chapter-I/subchapter-G/part-170/subpart-D/section-170.22)
affirmation under False Claims Act liability, and be wholly
non-conformant with a practice the same CIO office publishes as a
Top 10 basic for the DIB.

The score is not wrong. **The lens has a blind spot, and the
credential cannot see through it.**

- `AAT-01` — AI and autonomous technologies governance
- `AAT-02` — Model and prompt change management

Both are already among Mattermost's 45 in-scope controls, fired by
`ai-products: true` in the config, satisfying NIST AI RMF 1.0 and
ISO/IEC 42001:2023. **The control set can see this practice. The
800-171 view of that same control set cannot.**

That is the argument for one control set rendered into many
frameworks — made by the customer's own CIO, not by me.

It also lands exactly on [finding 03](../findings/#03--no-internal-ai-policy-while-publishing-ai-risk-guidance-for-other-people):
Mattermost publishes AI risk guidance for other organizations' CISOs
and has no published internal AI policy of its own — while selling
sovereign AI to the department that just published this list.

## Reachable is not the same as covered

Three practices resolve to a requirement that is materially weaker than
the practice itself. Counting them as clean would be the fail-open
defect this repo exists to catch.

**01 — Phishing-Resistant Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)**

Reachable, but not equivalent. 800-171 3.5.3 requires MFA; it does not require it to be phishing-resistant. A contractor using SMS OTP satisfies 3.5.3 in full and fails this practice outright. The requirement is a floor the practice has already moved past — worth naming in the interview, since it is the same shape as the AI gap below but subtler.

**07 — Integrate Security Early in the Development Lifecycle**

Barely reachable. 3.13.2 (architectural security principles, anchored to SA-8) is the only 800-171 requirement in the neighbourhood, and it is a design-principles statement, not a pipeline requirement. The DevSecOps substance of this practice lives in 800-53 SA-11/SA-15, which 800-171 r2 does not import. Flagged as thin rather than counted as clean.

**09 — Resilient Backup and Disaster Recovery Architecture**

Reachable but thin, in the same shape as practice 01. 3.8.9 requires backup confidentiality at storage locations. It does not require immutability, credential isolation, or restoration drills — the three things that actually defeat ransomware, and the three things this practice is entirely about. BCD-01 (business continuity and disaster recovery) carries the drill obligation; 800-171 does not reach BCD-01 at all.

## What this is not

This computes **reachability**, not conformance. Nothing here asserts
Mattermost's implementation state of any practice — that is knowable
only on day one, inside the boundary, from systems of record. Whether
the 800-171 lens can see a practice at all is knowable from public
documents, and that is the only thing computed here.

The OT Top 10 is deliberately unmapped: Mattermost has no operational
technology in its boundary, and mapping it would manufacture ten
findings out of nothing.
